Entering Your Interpretation

Activate the Enter Interpretation Screen

You begin entering your observations by clicking the mouse on the Enter Interpretation tab near the top of the Control Panel window. Data entry is divided into four separate subscreens. The data entry screen for each subscreen is activated by one of the large buttons on the left.

General Procedure

  1. Specify the image quality by clicking on the Indicate Image Quality button.
  2. EITHER click the Normal Study button to indicate that no abnormalities were found,
  3. OR
    1. Click on the Parenchymal Abnormalities button and enter the appropriate data.
    2. Click on the Pleural Abnormalities button and enter the appropriate data.
    3. Click on the Other Abnormalities button and enter the appropriate data.
  4. When all interpretation data have been entered, click on the Produce Report button.

Button Color Coding

Each of these buttons is initially displayed with a redish color to indicate that it has not yet been activated for the current study. When you click on a button it will turn gray to indicate that its data entry section has been visited. A button will turn green to indicate that you have entered sufficient data to complete that section.

Open the First Data Entry Screen: Image Quality

You open the first data entry screen by clicking the Indicate Image Quality button. It will turn gray to indicate that the image quality screen has been visited. Initially, the four image quality indicator buttons are displayed. Depending upon which one you select, additional data entry options may be displayed on this screen.

Describe the Image Quality

Click on button number 1 to indicate good image quality. In this case, no further information about image quality needs to be entered, so the color of the "Indicate Image Quality" button changes to green to indicate that this section has been completed.
If the image is less than optimal, then instead of selecting button "1", you would click on one of the buttons "2", "3" or "U/R". In this case, additional information about image quality needs to be entered, so additional options will be displayed.
If you select "Other", you will be asked to elaborate by entering descriptive information.

Indicate Parenchymal Abnormalities

This screen image shows a completed set of observations for the Parenchymal Abnormalities section.

Note that the color of the large "Parenchymal Abnormalities" button on the left is now green, indicating that this section has been completed. By the way, the four major data entry sections may be completed in any order you choose. The color of the large buttons will remind you which sections have not been completed.

Indicate Pleural Abnormalities

This screen image shows data entry in progress for the Pleural Abnormalities section.

If either the "R" or "L" button in the bottom row is clicked, additional data entry options will be displayed.

Indicate Other Abnormalities

This screen image shows data entry beginning for the Other Abnormalities section.

When this and the other three observation entry sections are complete, you proceed by clicking the white "Produce Report" button. This takes you to the next tab on the Control Panel, the "Produce Report" tab.

Entering Additional Information for the Report

This screen image shows sample data entry for the "Produce Report" tab.

After the information is entered, you click the "Show Report" button at the bottom of the screen. This causes the first page of the completed report form to be displayed.

The Report Display Window shows the first page of the completed two-page report form. Clicking on the "Show Page 2" button at the top of the window will cause the second page to be displayed. This button allows you to toggle back and forth between the two pages.

You can optionally magnify and pan this report display by using your mouse as you would for a subject study image. Clicking the "Reset Image" button will restore the original display size and position, just as it would for the subject study image.

Clicking on the "Print Report" button will cause the 2-page report form to be printed using the usual Windows printer options available on your computer workstation.

Report Approval and Study Conclusion

Here the "Approve Report" button has just been clicked. This action will close the current study. The pop-up question prompts you to delete the information for the study just completed. Clicking the "Approve Report" button represents your final sign-off on this study.

The approved report is saved in the C:\ProgramData\BViewer\Reports folder as a pair of .png image files. .png files are viewable using any common image viewing software. The approved report is added to the Currently Saved Reports list diisplayed on the right half of the Produce Report tab.
